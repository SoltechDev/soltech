General Information.

The General Certificate of Education (GCE) is a secondary school leaving qualification which includes two levels: Ordinary Level (O Level) and Advanced Level (A Level). It is offered by examination boards in various countries and is recognized globally.

The General Certificate of Education (GCE) consists of Ordinary Level (O Level) and Advanced Level (A Level) courses.

The General Certificate of Education (GCE) are subject-specific, allowing students to choose and specialize in various subjects. Each subject is assessed through written examinations, coursework, and practical assessments.

O Levels are foundational qualifications, covering basic concepts and skills in various subjects.

A Levels are more advanced, focusing on in-depth knowledge and understanding in specific subjects, often required for university entrance.
